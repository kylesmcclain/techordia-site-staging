A practical process before a proposal.

We look at how your team works, which systems matter, where risk sits, and what support path users need before recommending a model.

Support should leave the environment cleaner than it found it

The process is intentionally practical: understand the business, stabilize what interrupts work, document what matters, and keep improving the operating model.

01

Discover

Map users, devices, cloud systems, vendors, support paths, risks, and recurring pain.

02

Stabilize

Fix urgent gaps, clarify escalation, check backup visibility, and make core support tooling reliable.

03

Document

Capture users, devices, vendors, access, recurring fixes, and support notes where they belong.

04

Support

Handle tickets, maintenance, changes, and urgent issues with clear ownership.

05

Improve

Review lifecycle needs, security priorities, project opportunities, and preventable downtime.

What the process protects

Continuity

Support should reduce interruption, not create new dependency on one person or one undocumented workaround.

Clarity

Owners, users, vendors, systems, and escalation paths should be understandable before a problem becomes urgent.

Judgment

Good IT support uses technical depth, but the decision-making has to make sense to business operators.
